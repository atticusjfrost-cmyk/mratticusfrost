SUMMER MEMORIES

The sun rises up from the hills and I walk across the wildflower fields.

In the summers I would come here and lay down on the grass and watch the little lamblike clouds. The wildflowers are full of colour. They are pink and purple and yellow and white. They have always been. Bees and butterflies flap about, but again, they have always been here. When I lay down here, I can hear some sort of humming. No, less like a humming and more like a scream. The sky is blood red now. The sun is cut open and bleeding all out across the sky. Yes, I can hear it. I tremble with anxiety as I hear a terrible scream passes through all of memory.
