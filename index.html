Mind-Rabbit Hole 1

The town I grew up in did not get much rain. Each year, it rained less and less. 500mm. 300mm. Then 100mm. Then 50mm. Finally, we hit 0mm. It was a farming town.

I remember the town meetings we had about it. What to do and what caused it. It was always a big debate. I never liked going. One time this old burly man, Llewyn, I think his name was, stood up and said enough is enough. He said that the drought was because we’d gone and pissed off God and that we’d let evil cheerfully skip into our town with all this machinery. He said it was a silver serpent under a flower, ready to strike. The following night I prayed. I prayed so hard my nose bled but I asked for rain. It didn’t come the next day. Or the one after. On the third day it did rain. The fourth and fifth too. It rained so hard the streets had turned to rivers and all the clouds had burst open. With no other choice, we left.

It wasn’t until much later, 20 or 30 years I think, that there was a commission into some shady practices of the coalition. It was found that they had induced heavy rainfall on many off-world farming colonies, in attempt to get more people into the main planets and cities.
